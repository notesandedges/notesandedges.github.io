I don't want to go to college. Whenever I realize how close it's getting, all I can think isâ€¦fac.

Which is probably why I'm spending so much of my time doing things, making things, learning things. It's my distraction from all of the changes happening around me: our weekend shopping trips to pick up bedside lamps, laundry baskets, new clothes, the invitations and reminders I get in the mail from my college every other week, my parents' constant lectures about staying fit and focused, as if they're cramming all of their wisdom into these last months before I leave.

Every time I'm with my little brother or visit my younger cousins, I find myself thinking about what people they're going to grow up to be: jocks? wannabes? leaders? followers? What will they do in high school, where will they go to college?

Every time I visit my aunts and uncles, I find myself in the midst of animated discussions about politics and the declining field of health care, too ignorant to participate, only watchful of the storm brewing: what problems will our generation inherit? I am bombarded with the obvious "are you ready?" as if there's nothing else to say about such a momentous shift.

Because it is momentous, not just in the sense that I'll be doing my own laundry, or even that I'll be away from my parents and friends and finding my way in a completely new environment. It's realizing that I'm all I'll ever have, that adults too have limitations.

It's a time where your thoughts have no happy place. I try not to look back, nor to the future, but that's difficult because when you're like me, every moment of the present is spent thinking about how I could be spending it more efficiently. But what else is there to think about besides a limbo of shafts of sunlight, the smell of bread my dad makes every other Sunday as he transitions into old–people–hood, the thunder of the rain on the roof of our house, the inexplicable absence of rainbows?

It's also a time to enjoy the summer as it's supposed to be. This week I made french toast with blueberry syrup and cottage cheese that tasted horrible (because I don't like french toast much anyway, but our pancake batter had expired), went rollerblading, narrowly escaped being struck by lightning after a trip to the beach, where I buried myself in the sand and let the fine silica grains rub away at my dead skin, finished reading Stuff Matters, reread two YA series in just a few days, among other things to make the most of my childhood before it ends.
